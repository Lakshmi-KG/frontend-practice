Pseudo-Element-Selectors Example:

Pseudo-element selectors in CSS are used to style specific parts of an element rather than the entire element. They allow developers to apply styles to the first letter, first line, or selected text, and also to insert content before or after an element. Pseudo-elements help improve design and presentation without changing the HTML structure.
